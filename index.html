IP Address FAQs

Got questions about your IP address? We've got you covered.

An IP address (Internet Protocol address) is a unique identifier assigned to your device when you connect to the internet. It allows websites and services to communicate with your device.

No, your IP address can reveal your approximate location (city or region), but it cannot pinpoint your exact street address or identity without help from your ISP or legal authorities.

You can use a VPN (Virtual Private Network), proxy server, or the Tor browser to mask your IP address and improve your online privacy.

If your ISP uses dynamic IPs (which most do), your IP address can change over time or when you reconnect. A static IP remains fixed and requires specific setup or purchase.

IPv4 addresses look like 192.0.2.1 and are the older, more common format. IPv6 addresses, like 2001:0db8::1, were created to handle the growing number of internet-connected devices.

Websites use your IP address to deliver content, tailor experiences to your region, detect suspicious activity, serve ads, and gather analytics about visitors.
